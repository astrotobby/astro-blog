<p> In a move that has sent shockwaves through the dev community, GitHub has officially retired its flat-rate monthly subscription for Copilot in favor of a <b data-index-in-node="153" data-path-to-node="19">Per-Token Pricing</b> model.</p><p data-path-to-node="20"><b data-index-in-node="0" data-path-to-node="20">Why the change?</b>
As models like GPT-5 and Mythos become more expensive to run, the "all-you-can-eat" $10–$20/month model became unsustainable for high-compute tasks.</p><p data-path-to-node="21"><b data-index-in-node="0" data-path-to-node="21">What you need to know:</b></p><ul data-path-to-node="22"><li><p data-path-to-node="22,0,0"><b data-index-in-node="0" data-path-to-node="22,0,0">Pay for Precision:</b> Developers will now be billed based on the complexity and length of the code generated.</p></li><li><p data-path-to-node="22,1,0"><b data-index-in-node="0" data-path-to-node="22,1,0">The "Efficiency" Incentive:</b> This move encourages developers to write cleaner, more modular prompts to save on costs.</p></li><li><p data-path-to-node="22,2,0"><b data-index-in-node="0" data-path-to-node="22,2,0">Enterprise Tiers:</b> Large companies can still opt for "Reserved Capacity" blocks, but for the independent dev, your monthly bill will now fluctuate based on your actual output.</p></li></ul><p data-path-to-node="23">While some are frustrated by the lack of predictability, GitHub argues this allows them to integrate the world's most powerful (and expensive) models directly into your IDE without compromising performance.</p><p data-path-to-node="24"><br /></p>
