# Google I/O 2026: Every Major Announcement That Changes How We Use AI

Google just wrapped one of the most packed developer conferences in its history. Google I/O 2026, held May 19–20 at Shoreline Amphitheatre in Mountain View, was wall-to-wall AI — and this time the announcements weren't just incremental upgrades. Google dropped new models, a new agentic development platform, a redesigned shopping experience, intelligent eyewear, and tools that fundamentally change how developers build software.

Here's every major announcement broken down clearly — no fluff.

---

## 1. Gemini Omni — The Most Powerful Google Model Yet

The headline model of I/O 2026 is **Gemini Omni**, a new multimodal model that accepts any input — image, audio, video, or text — and produces a single cohesive output. The launch focus is video creation: you can feed Gemini Omni a selfie, a text prompt, or a reference video and it will generate and edit video using conversational language.

Key capabilities:
- Applies cinematic effects like zoom changes, background swaps, and style transformations via simple prompts
- Supports real-world grounded generation, meaning outputs are tied to factual context, not hallucinated scenes
- **Gemini Omni Flash** is already rolling out today to all AI Plus, Pro, and Ultra subscribers via the Gemini app and Google Flow — also available free (18+) in YouTube Shorts Remix and YouTube Create

This is Google's direct counter to OpenAI's Sora and Runway ML, and by all accounts it's a serious one.

---

## 2. Gemini 3.5 Flash — Speed Meets Action

**Gemini 3.5 Flash** is the first model in Google's new 3.5 series, and it's built differently from past Gemini versions. The key upgrade: it combines frontier intelligence with **action**. It's not just answering questions — it's executing tasks.

Benchmark highlights:
- **4x faster** output speed compared to predecessor models
- Beats competing models on speed while maintaining reasoning quality
- Already rolling out in Antigravity 2.0, the Gemini API, and Google AI Studio

**Gemini 3.5 Pro** is currently in testing and drops next month.

---

## 3. Google Antigravity 2.0 — The Agent-First Development Platform

This is the biggest shift for developers. **Google Antigravity** (formerly a Google Labs project) is now Google's flagship agent-first development platform — and version 2.0 is a major leap.

What's new in Antigravity 2.0:
- **Antigravity CLI**: A new command-line interface letting you spin up specialized subagents to tackle complex coding workflows
- Built-in cross-platform terminal sandboxing, credential masking, and hardened Git policies for security
- **Managed Agents**: A single API call now gives you a fully provisioned agent with a remote sandbox — zero infrastructure setup required
- **Antigravity SDK**: Programmatic control over the agent harness so you can deploy it on your own infrastructure
- Baked directly into **Google Search** for AI subscribers, letting users build custom apps inside their browser

Antigravity can now help developers build entire operating systems. Google demonstrated it running Doom during the keynote.

---

## 4. Google AI Studio — Now Builds Full-Stack Apps

**Google AI Studio** got a major upgrade that turns it from a prototyping tool into a full-stack deployment platform:

- **Native Kotlin support** for Android app development (vibe coding Android apps directly in the browser)
- **Google Workspace integrations** built in
- **One-click deploy to Cloud Run** with Firebase services support
- Full project export to Antigravity for continued development

This means you can go from idea → working Android app → deployed backend without leaving AI Studio.

---

## 5. Gemini Spark — Your 24/7 Personal AI Agent

**Gemini Spark** is Google's answer to a persistent personal AI agent. It lives in the Gemini app and handles your digital life proactively — checking emails, managing tasks, setting reminders, and acting on your behalf across Google products.

**Android Halo** complements Spark: a subtle system-level UI at the top of your screen that shows what your agent is working on at any given time, without interrupting whatever you're doing. Rolling out later this year.

**Daily Brief** also launched inside the Gemini app — a personalized daily digest curated by your agent.

---

## 6. Universal Cart — AI-Powered Shopping Across Every Platform

**Universal Cart** is one of the most practically useful announcements. It's a Gemini-powered intelligent shopping cart that works across the Gemini app, YouTube, and Gmail.

How it works:
- Add products from multiple retailers into one unified cart
- Gemini agents automatically track price drops, price history, and stock alerts
- If you're building something complex (like a custom PC with parts from 5 different stores), Universal Cart aggregates everything and hunts deals automatically

Launching in the US first this summer. This is agentic commerce in practice — not just a concept.

---

## 7. Information Agents in Search — Always-On Search Results

Google Search is entering what the company calls a **new AI Search stage**. **Information Agents** run in the background 24/7 and send you alerts and notifications when your search query gets matched with new information.

Use cases shown:
- Following a creator's latest content drops
- Monitoring stock values in real time
- Tracking news topics you care about

This essentially turns Google Search into a live subscription service for any topic you care about.

---

## 8. Ask YouTube — Conversational Video Search

**Ask YouTube** entirely reimagines how you find and navigate video content. Instead of typing keywords and scrolling thumbnails, you ask conversational questions and get direct answers sourced from video content.

This is a significant shift for content creators — it means discoverability will increasingly depend on what's *said* in your video, not just the title and tags.

---

## 9. WebMCP — AI Agents Now Work Inside Browsers

**WebMCP** is a proposed open web standard that exposes structured tools (JavaScript functions, HTML forms) to browser-based AI agents, so they can execute complex tasks with greater speed and precision.

- Experimental origin trial starts in **Chrome 149**
- Gemini in Chrome support coming soon
- Chrome DevTools for Agents also launched — agents can now automate quality audits, emulate user experiences, and debug code in real time without manual oversight

For web developers, this means your apps need to be agent-readable, not just human-readable.

---

## 10. Intelligent Eyewear — Smart Glasses Are Back

Google partnered with **Samsung** to launch **Intelligent Eyewear** — smart glasses with Gemini built in. Announced live at I/O, they're arriving this fall.

Features include:
- Real-time directions without pulling out your phone
- Send texts by voice
- Snap photos
- Contextual AI overlay powered by Gemini

Google also has separate Gemini-powered frames launching with their own feature set.

---

## 11. Googlebooks — Android Laptops Are Here

Announced at the pre-show Android Show on May 12, **Googlebooks** is a new premium laptop category powered by Android, with partners including Acer, ASUS, and Lenovo. First units ship this fall.

Standout features:
- Gemini baked in at the OS level
- Full Android app support
- Phone app streaming (mirror your phone apps on the laptop)
- Magic Pointer and Create My Widget tools
- Material 3 Expressive design language

---

## 12. Google Workspace Gets Voice Everything

New **voice capabilities** are rolling out across Gmail, Google Docs, and Keep:
- Dictate drafts in Gmail and Docs by speaking
- Take notes in Keep hands-free
- Contextual commands like "summarize the last hour of the meeting" or "add a reminder for tomorrow"
- AI Inbox updates for smarter email management

A new design tool called **Google Pics** also launched as part of the Workspace expansion.

---

## 13. Developer Tools That Change How Android Apps Are Built

For developers specifically, Google shipped several powerful new tools:

- **Android CLI & Skills (stable)**: Lets AI agents tap directly into Android Studio's heavy-lifting capabilities — downloading SDKs, running apps on devices, executing complex workflows
- **Migration Agent**: Converts React Native, web frameworks, or iOS apps to native Kotlin Android apps automatically. Migrations that previously took weeks now take hours
- **Android Bench**: Google's LLM leaderboard for Android development tasks — now includes open-weight models like Gemma 4
- **Modern Web Guidance**: Expert-vetted skills for coding agents covering 100+ use cases for performant, accessible, secure web apps
- **HTML-in-Canvas API**: Lets developers integrate real DOM elements into WebGL/WebGPU canvases — fully searchable, accessible, and interactive

---

## 14. AI Ultra Plan — $100/Month for Power Users

Google launched a **new $100/month AI Ultra plan** aimed at developers, technical leads, knowledge workers, and advanced creators. It includes:
- 5x higher usage limits in the Gemini app and Antigravity vs the AI Pro plan
- 20TB of cloud storage
- Priority access to new features and frontier models

---

## 15. Build with Gemini XPRIZE Hackathon — $2M Prize Pool

Google announced a global developer competition with a **$2 million prize pool** — the largest hackathon prize ever. The challenge: use Gemini to build real applications solving the world's most pressing problems. Details at io.google.

## What This All Means

The through-line at Google I/O 2026 is clear: **we've moved from AI that assists to AI that acts**. Every announcement — from Antigravity to Gemini Spark to Universal Cart to Information Agents — is about agents doing work on your behalf, not just answering questions.

For creators and developers, the implications are significant:
- **Android apps** can now be built and deployed with minimal code knowledge via AI Studio and Antigravity
- **Content on YouTube** will be surfaced conversationally through Ask YouTube — optimize your audio content, not just your titles
- **Web development** is shifting to agent-readable architecture via WebMCP
- **Shopping and commerce** are being restructured around AI agents that track deals 24/7

Google isn't playing catch-up anymore. I/O 2026 signals the company is now building the infrastructure layer for the agentic era — and the pace is accelerating.
